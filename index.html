FAQ

What is an Marsol?

▾

An Marsol is a unique number assigned to each satoshi based on the order it was mined. It's like giving each tiny piece of Bitcoin its own special ID number. Marsol inscriptions are when you embed data like text, hashes, JSON code, or even images onto a satoshi. It's like giving your Bitcoin a little tattoo to make it stand out from the crowd.

What is project?

▾

Marsol are unique digital collectibles on the Solana blockchain, like rare digital art or media. They're similar to Bitcoin Ordinals but built on the Dogecoin network.

How is it different from an Memecoin?

▾

Compared to traditional Memecoin, one major advantage of Marsol is decentralized and higher security standards. Thanks to the cryptographic properties of Bitcoin and Doge.

Ordinals allow metadata to be recorded on satoshis, ensuring that Ordinals are consistently stored on the Bitcoin and Doge blockchains. This core feature makes them more decentralized and secure. In contrast, most NFT types currently found in the NFT market are stored on decentralized applications using self-executing smart contracts like EVM. This reliance on contract-encoded rules and logic makes traditional NFTs dependent on the contract layer.
